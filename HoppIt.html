Overview

The
Brand

The client approached me with a single brief: build a brand for a sportswear line. No name, no direction — just a product category and a target audience of Gen-Z urban athletes in Tirupur.

The name HoppIt was my decision. Short for Hopp-It — Quick Wear. The idea: wear it quick, move fast. It represents speed, agility, and ease — a sportswear brand you just throw on and go. The natural mascot for that energy? A rabbit. Fast, energetic, and immediately recognisable.

The challenge was making it premium without losing personality. The solution: refined display typography anchoring the wordmark, with a characterful bunny sitting on top of it — funny, bold, and confident at the same time. The concept was revised with a senior designer and approved by the client without corrections.

01
The Challenge
Starting From Zero
No existing brand elements. No mood reference. The client had a product category and a target audience — nothing else. The challenge was to define the personality, visual language, and identity system entirely through research and design thinking before a single line was drawn.
02
The Goal
A System, Not Just a Logo
Deliver a complete, scalable brand identity system — logo variants, mascot, colour palette, typography rules, and usage guidelines — that any team member could apply consistently without needing a designer present.
Design Process

How It
Was Built

01
Research
Market & Competitor Study
Studied 6 activewear brands operating in the Indian market — Nike India, Puma, Vector X, Wildcraft, ASICS, and Decathlon. Mapped their visual language, colour choices, mascot usage, and typography. Identified the gap: most Indian activewear brands either copy global aesthetics or feel too regional. HoppIt needed to own a space between the two.
02
Concept
Brand Personality & Mood
Defined three brand personality pillars: Energetic · Confident · Accessible. Built a moodboard around dynamic motion, urban textures, and bold colour blocking. The name "HoppIt" implied movement, agility, and a slight playfulness — which pointed toward a mascot-forward identity.
03
Logo Sketches
Ideation & Rough Exploration
Started with 20+ rough sketches on paper — exploring lettermark, wordmark, and icon combinations. Explored motion trails, jumping silhouettes, geometric H-marks, and mascot-integrated logos. Narrowed to 4 directions for digital development.

Final Logo
The Chosen Direction
