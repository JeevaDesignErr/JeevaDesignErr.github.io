The Philosophy
01
WHY I CALL MYSELF
A DESIGNERR
It's not a typo — it's who I am. I didn't go to a design school. I learned by opening Adobe, making something terrible, figuring out why it was terrible, and trying again. Every error taught me something a classroom never could. That's the honest truth behind the name DesignErr.
02
I STUDIED MATHS
NOT DESIGN
Funny thing — I actually wanted Computer Science, but financial constraints pushed me into Mathematics. Turned out to be the best accident of my life. Grids, proportions, type scales — they're all just geometry. While most designers feel this intuitively, I can tell you exactly why it works. Maths gave me the precision; errors gave me the instinct.
03
I BUILD SYSTEMS,
NOT JUST VISUALS
When I take on a brand project, I'm not just making a logo. I'm thinking about how it looks on a jersey label, an Instagram post, a packaging box, and a business card — all at once. If the system doesn't hold together across every touchpoint, the job isn't done. That obsession with consistency is what separates a logo from a brand.
04
EVERYTHING I DO
IS STORYTELLING
Whether I'm designing a mascot, animating a 3D scene, or laying out a restaurant menu — I'm always asking the same question: what does this make you feel before you read a single word? That's the skill. The software is just the tool. The story is always the work.
